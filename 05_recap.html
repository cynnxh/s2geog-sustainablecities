Question 1 · 4 marks
Explain how rapid urban population growth can lead to competition for natural resources in cities.
🔵 Model Answer Land: A growing population needs more land for housing, so low-rise buildings may be replaced by high-rise ones as density increases; this can leave less space for green/agricultural land and cause conflict when land is limited.

Water: A growing population needs a continual supply of water. Where land is limited, this constrains the building or expansion of reservoirs due to competing housing, transport and industrial needs. As more natural vegetation is converted into roads and concrete, water catchment and groundwater are reduced, causing water levels in lakes and rivers to decline and increasing the rate of evaporation.
Question 2 · 3 marks
Name one urban problem a city is likely to face, and explain how it can create a problem for the community.
🔵 Model Answer (example: Air Pollution) One urban problem is air pollution. Cities rely heavily on fossil fuels for electricity generation, factories and vehicles, releasing harmful gases such as carbon dioxide, sulphur dioxide and nitrogen oxides. Long-term exposure to these pollutants can cause respiratory illnesses (e.g. asthma, lung cancer) and heart disease among residents, increasing strain on healthcare services and reducing the community's overall quality of life.
Question 3 · 3 marks
Explain how Singapore has managed water pollution at Kranji Reservoir.
🔵 Model Answer Kranji Reservoir is located near Singapore's main agricultural area, where rainwater mixing with excess fertiliser from farms caused polluted run-off to flow into the reservoir. To manage this, cut-off drains were constructed around the farms to channel the polluted surface run-off into treatment ponds instead of directly into the reservoir. Plants such as pandan and cattails were grown on floating wetlands at the edge of these ponds to help naturally purify the water, improving the water quality of the reservoir over time.
Question 4 · Calculation Practice
A city has a population of 5.8 million and a land area of 720 km². Calculate its population density.
people/km²
🔵 Model Answer Population density = population ÷ land area
= 5.8 million ÷ 720 km²
= 5,800,000 ÷ 720
≈ 8,056 people/km²
Question 5 · 4 marks
Explain why cities are able to grow rapidly.
🔵 Model Answer Cities grow rapidly due to the concentration of activities and a large number of jobs, which attracts skilled and educated workers (rural-urban and urban-to-urban migration). Institutes of higher learning found in cities help develop a skilled workforce, and the resulting knowledge economy attracts investors and entrepreneurs, creating an ideal environment for innovation. This cycle of opportunity draws even more people to migrate into cities, causing further growth.
